# Campaign Brief: Innovate South 2026 Recap

## Campaign Overview

Campaign name: Innovate South 2026 Recap Launch

Campaign type: Event recap, ecosystem storytelling, founder and partner activation

Primary asset: https://henrytylerhebert-eng.github.io/innovate-south-2026-recap/

Owner: Opportunity Machine / Innovate South team

Primary channels:

- Mailchimp newsletter
- OM social channels
- Partner and stakeholder shares
- Direct follow-up to founders, mentors, investors, and civic partners

## Objective

Use the Innovate South recap to make the Lafayette founder ecosystem visible, useful, and shareable after the event.

The campaign should help people see:

- What happened at Innovate South.
- Which founders, investors, mentors, and partners showed up.
- What practical ideas founders can use.
- How Opportunity Machine and LEDA support founder-first economic development.
- Where to go next for programs, events, mentorship, jobs, and community support.

## Audience

Primary:

- Founders and aspiring founders
- Innovate South attendees
- Opportunity Machine members and alumni

Secondary:

- Mentors and investors
- LEDA and civic/economic development partners
- University and workforce partners
- Regional tech community
- Sponsors and ecosystem supporters

## Positioning

Core message:

Innovate South is not just an event recap. It is a snapshot of a founder-first ecosystem learning how to turn relationships, capital, talent, and community into company-building momentum.

Voice:

- Founder-first
- Practical
- Editorial
- Regional and proud, without sounding provincial
- Premium but not corporate

Avoid:

- Generic hype
- Unsupported impact claims
- Treating attendance, clicks, or visuals as proof of founder outcomes
- Overexplaining internal OM systems in public copy

## Newsletter Structure

Use the OM Startup Scoop format and current Mailchimp header/footer pattern.

Recommended modules:

1. Hero: Innovate South 2026 recap is live
2. Opening Note: Mandi Mitchell, Destin Ortego, and founder-first economic development
3. AI-Native Culture: Jeremiah Owyang keynote
4. Capital: investor panel and Vlad Cazacu
5. Community: Network Effect and tech meetups
6. Tech Tank: Mirror Labs, XR MediX, and the pitch-stage founders
7. Closing CTA: read, share, and send to a founder or partner

## Success Measures

Track as campaign performance, not proof of founder outcomes:

- Email opens
- Email clicks
- Clicks to the full recap
- Clicks to specific recap sections
- Shares from partners or stakeholders
- Replies with founder/partner follow-up opportunities
- New programming or mentorship conversations triggered by the recap

Founder or ecosystem outcomes should only be promoted later if reviewed and sourced.

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Incorrect speaker/company attribution | Verify against transcripts, source docs, and reviewed names before send |
| Photo/name mismatch | Use approved recap image names and captions |
| Overclaiming impact | Keep campaign metrics separate from evidence of founder outcomes |
| Canva image-only details | Put key details and links in live email text |
| Broken recap hash links | Test every CTA in Mailchimp preview and live browser |
